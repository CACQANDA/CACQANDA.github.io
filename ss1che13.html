Terms

Hydrocarbons: organic compounds made of only carbon and hydrogen atoms

Alkanes: a type of hydrocarbon with only single bonds between carbon atoms

Alkenes: a type of hydrocarbon with at least one double bond between carbon atoms

Alkynes: a type of hydrocarbon with at least one triple bond between carbon atoms

Aromatic hydrocarbons: a type of hydrocarbon with a ring structure containing alternating single and double bonds between carbon atoms

Hydrocarbons are organic compounds made of only carbon and hydrogen atoms. They are the primary component of fossil fuels such as coal, oil and natural gas. Hydrocarbons can be classified into different types based on the types of bonds between carbon atoms.

Alkanes are a type of hydrocarbon with only single bonds between carbon atoms. They are also known as saturated hydrocarbons and have the general formula CnH2n+2. Methane (CH4) and propane (C3H8) are examples of alkanes.

Alkenes are a type of hydrocarbon with at least one double bond between carbon atoms. They are also known as unsaturated hydrocarbons and have the general formula CnH2n. Ethene (C2H4) and propene (C3H6) are examples of alkenes.

Alkynes are a type of hydrocarbon with at least one triple bond between carbon atoms. They have the general formula CnH2n-2. Ethyne (C2H2) and propyne (C3H4) are examples of alkynes.

Aromatic hydrocarbons are a type of hydrocarbon with a ring structure containing alternating single and double bonds between carbon atoms. Examples of aromatic hydrocarbons include benzene (C6H6) and toluene (C7H8). These compounds are characterized by their strong, distinct odors and are often used in the production of chemicals such as dyes and pesticides.

Hydrocarbons are important in the energy industry as they are the primary source of fuel for transportation and electricity generation. They are also used as raw materials in the chemical industry to produce a wide range of products such as plastics, solvents, and fertilizers. The extraction, transportation, and burning of hydrocarbons can have a significant impact on the environment, and it is important to use these resources responsibly and develop sustainable alternatives.
